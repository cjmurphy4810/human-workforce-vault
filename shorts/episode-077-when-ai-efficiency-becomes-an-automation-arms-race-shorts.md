# 10 Short-Form Content Ideas

## Idea 1: The Automation Paradox

**Hook:** "Your company saves $1M by replacing workers with AI—then wonders why customers can't afford to buy anything."

**Key Message:** Individual automation decisions are rational; collective automation destroys the consumer demand that sustains all businesses.

**Suggested Visual:** Split screen—left side shows cost savings graph going up, right side shows customer purchasing power going down; animated collision at center.

**CTA:** "Watch the full episode to see why the automation arms race hurts everyone."

---

## Idea 2: The Wage That Does Two Jobs

**Hook:** "Those wages were rent, groceries, and school shoes. But spreadsheets only see one thing: cost."

**Key Message:** Worker salaries function simultaneously as business expenses and as demand drivers for the entire economy; cutting one destroys both.

**Suggested Visual:** Salary broken into visual components (housing, food, utilities, spending) flowing into different economic sectors; then showing collapse when wage is cut.

**CTA:** "Learn why wages are demand—link in bio."

---

## Idea 3: The Temporal Mismatch

**Hook:** "History says work gets reshaped, not destroyed. But families can't eat history. They need rent money *now*."

**Key Message:** Even if workers eventually find new roles, the displacement-to-reabsorption gap spans years during which families face real hardship.

**Suggested Visual:** Timeline showing layoff → job search → retraining → new role; highlighting the gap with calendar pages falling; family silhouette on income cliff.

**CTA:** "See how companies are solving the timing problem."

---

## Idea 4: Shinier, Emptier Shops

**Hook:** "You automate, cut costs, boost productivity. Then your customers disappear. Welcome to deadweight loss."

**Key Message:** Over-automation harms not just workers but business owners themselves through demand destruction and revenue erosion.

**Suggested Visual:** Sleek, modern automated store or office with cutting-edge tech but empty—no customers, no activity, no demand.

**CTA:** "Find out why efficiency without demand is a losing game."

---

## Idea 5: What AI Should Actually Replace

**Hook:** "Stop replacing people. Start replacing drudgery."

**Key Message:** The transformative opportunity is automating administrative burden (triage, scheduling, compliance) while preserving the uniquely human work (judgment, empathy, presence, care).

**Suggested Visual:** Doctor/teacher/manager doing paperwork and admin tasks fades out; same person providing care, mentoring, presence appears bright and emphasized.

**CTA:** "See how to design AI that elevates human work."

---

## Idea 6: What Machines Can't See

**Hook:** "A machine flags performance. A teacher sees shame, boredom, fear, talent, spark. Those are different universes."

**Key Message:** Judgment, interpretation, emotional intelligence, and contextual understanding remain uniquely human—and increasingly valuable.

**Suggested Visual:** Split screen showing algorithmic flagging (cold, metrics-based) vs. human observation (warm, intuitive, contextual); emphasize the difference.

**CTA:** "Learn which jobs AI can't actually do."

---

## Idea 7: The Collective Action Problem

**Hook:** "Every company knows automation will hurt demand. Every company does it anyway. That's not stupidity—it's a trap."

**Key Message:** Competitive pressure forces firms to automate defensively, even when executives recognize systemic risk; this is a structural problem requiring governance, not individual virtue.

**Suggested Visual:** Multiple companies animated as players in a race, moving toward automation edge; visual showing they're all trapped by incentive structure, not choice.

**CTA:** "See how policy can change the incentives."

---

## Idea 8: The 100,000 Question

**Hook:** "100,000+ tech workers laid off in 2025. And that's just the beginning. Here's what happens next."

**Key Message:** With 80% of US jobs potentially affected by LLMs, workforce displacement is systemic—not cyclical—and requires rethinking how we approach work redesign and reabsorption.

**Suggested Visual:** Large number (100,000) on screen; ripple effect showing impact across tech roles; then expanding rings showing vulnerable job categories.

**CTA:** "See the full impact analysis and what companies are doing differently."

---

## Idea 9: Role Redesign, Not Elimination

**Hook:** "Reframe the question: Not 'How do we replace this role?' but 'How do we eliminate the busywork from it?'"

**Key Message:** High-performing AI implementations focus on automating procedural tasks while preserving and amplifying human-centered judgment, creativity, and mentorship.

**Suggested Visual:** Job description breaking apart; procedural tasks (scheduling, compliance, data entry) move to AI system; human tasks (mentoring, judgment, empathy) highlighted and magnified.

**CTA:** "See examples of AI augmentation that works."

---

## Idea 10: The Fork in the Road

**Hook:** "AI gives us back time, focus, and capability. Do we use that dividend on *more* human work, or just *fewer* humans? That's the question."

**Key Message:** The future of work isn't determined by technology—it's determined by the choices leaders make about whether automation serves human dignity or just corporate spreadsheets.

**Suggested Visual:** Road diverging—left path shows smaller, more capable teams doing richer work; right path shows automated systems with skeleton crew; final frame asks viewer which path.

**CTA:** "Vote with your leadership—choose human-centered AI."
